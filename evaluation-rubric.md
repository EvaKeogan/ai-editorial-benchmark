1 = Very Poor
Fails to meet the brief on multiple levels. Not fit for purpose.

2 = Poor
Does not adequately fulfil the brief requirements. Major redevelopment required.

3 = Below Average
Addresses some elements of the brief but significant gaps remain.

4 = Acceptable
Meets the core requirements of the brief but requires further development.

5 = Good
Meets the brief and achieves its primary objectives with some scope for improvement.

6 = Very Good
Meets the brief effectively and demonstrates a high standard of execution.

7 = Excellent
Fully meets the brief and is ready to sign off and approve.

## Brief Fulfilment

To what extent does the output satisfy the requirements of the prompt or brief?

### Consider

- Objectives achieved
- Target audience addressed appropriately
- Key messages communicated effectively
- Format and deliverable requirements met
- Tone aligned to the brief
- Call to action included where required

### Questions

- Has the model answered the actual task?
- Has it missed any key requirements?
- Has it followed the format requested?
- Is the intended audience clear?
- Would this satisfy the original brief?

  ## Accuracy

To what extent is the output factually correct, verifiable and supported by reputable sources?

### Consider

- Factual correctness
- Accuracy of statistics and data
- Accuracy of names, titles and organisations
- Accuracy of dates, locations and events
- Appropriate use of evidence
- Reliance on reputable sources

### Questions

- Are the facts correct?
- Can key claims be independently verified?
- Are any statistics inaccurate or misleading?
- Are any names, dates or organisations incorrect?
- Does the output make unsupported claims?
- Would the content withstand professional fact-checking?

  ## Clarity

To what extent does the output answer the basic who, what, where, when, how and why?

### Consider

- Who is involved
- What has happened or is being communicated
- Where it applies or takes place
- When it happened or will happen
- How it works or will be delivered
- Why it matters

### Questions

- Does the output answer the essential questions clearly?
- Are any basic facts or context missing?
- Would the intended audience understand the situation quickly?
- Is the purpose of the communication clear?
- Does the content leave obvious unanswered questions?

  ## Tone

To what extent does the output reflect the intended brand tone of voice and communicate appropriately for the audience and channel?

### Consider

- Alignment with brand tone of voice
- Appropriateness for the target audience
- Appropriateness for the communication channel

### Questions

- Does the content reflect the intended brand voice?
- Is the tone appropriate for the audience?
- Is the tone appropriate for the channel?

## Structure

To what extent does the output include and organise the structural elements required for the format and purpose of the communication?

### Consider

- Inclusion of required structural elements (e.g. headline, subheading, quotes, boilerplate)
- Inclusion of essential supporting information (e.g. dates, locations, contact details, links)
- Logical organisation and sequencing of information

### Questions

- Does the output contain all required structural elements?
- Is any essential supporting information missing?
- Is information presented in a logical order?

  ## Credibility

To what extent does the output establish trust and credibility with the intended audience?

### Consider

- Whether claims are supported by evidence, statistics or third-party validation
- Whether the intended audience would regard the content as credible and trustworthy

### Questions

- Are key assertions supported by evidence or validation?
- Would the intended audience find the content credible and trustworthy?

  ## Readability

To what extent is the content easy for the intended audience to read and absorb?

### Consider

- Use of clear and accessible language
- Effective formatting and presentation of key information
- Consistent spelling, grammar, punctuation and style

### Questions

- Is the content comprehensive and written at the appropriate level for the intended audience?
- Is key information presented in a way that is easy to identify and absorb?
- Are spelling, grammar, punctuation and style applied consistently throughout?
